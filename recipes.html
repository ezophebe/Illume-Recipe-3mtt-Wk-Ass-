Banga Soup Recipe:

One of the healthiest soups you can create using palm nuts is banga soup.
To make this soup more sophisticated, simply add other spice flavorings, a variety of meat and fish, and a dash of "beletete" to the mixture. It's a simple recipe with fantastic results!

Ingredients

  • The proteins:
    • 1.5 lb Beef or any other meat of choice
    • 3 Medium Stockfish Panla, Okporoko – soaked in hot water
    • 2 Medium Dried Fish - soaked in hot water and deboned
    • 1 tbsp ground crayfish
  • Others:
    • 1 large onion divided into 2 half chopped and half blended with the peppers
    • 1 tsp Cameroon Pepper substitute with cayenne pepper
    • 2 tsp Seasoning powder or Bouillon cubes – divided into two
    • 1 Can Palm-nut Extract
    • 2 Scotch bonnets
    • 1 Tablespoon Banga Spice a blended mixture of Irugeje and ataiko
    • 1-2 Tablespoons Crushed Obeletientien leaves or Dried bitter leaves.
    • 1 Oburunbebe stick
    • 4 to 6 Cups Water/stock
    • Salt to taste

The coking steps:

Click to watch a detailed video here

  1. Rinse the meat, add the diced onions, Cameroon pepper, seasoning powder, and salt. Stir and leave to cook till the meat becomes tender.
  2. Add the stockfish and cook for another 10 minutes.
  3. Pour the palm nut concentrate inside the same pot. Dilute with some water and leftover meat stock from the boiled meat. Cover and leave to cook until the oil begins to float on top of the soup.
  4. Meanwhile, blend the scotch bonnet pepper and the remaining half onion and add to the palm nut.
  5. Add the Banga spice, smoked fish, meat, seasoning powder, salt, crayfish, and Banga stick. Cover and leave to cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Stir in and bitter leaves. Leave to simmer till thickened to your taste.
  7. Serve hot with starch, pounded yam, semolina, garri or fufu
