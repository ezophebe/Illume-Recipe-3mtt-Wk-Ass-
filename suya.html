Nigeria Suya Recipe:

Suya is a popular Nigerian street food made with thinly sliced beef, chicken, or ram meat, marinated in a spicy peanut-based seasoning and grilled over an open flame or in an oven. It is delicious, smoky, and perfect for a quick meal or gathering.

Ingredients

  • The suya spice (Yagi Spice):
    • 3 tablespoons ground roasted peanuts (kuli-kuli or peanut powder)
    • 1 tablespoon paprika (for color)
    • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust for heat)
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon ginger powder
    • 1 teaspoon onion powder
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ½ teaspoon white pepper (optional)
    • 1 seasoning cube (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Others:
    • For the Meat:
    • 500g beef, chicken, or ram meat (thinly sliced)
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 8-10 skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes if using wooden skewers)
    • For Garnishing:
    • 1 medium onion (sliced into rings)
    • 1 medium tomato (sliced)
    • 1 medium cucumber (sliced)
    • Extra suya spice for serving

The coking steps:

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Suya Spice
    • If using whole peanuts, blend them into a fine powder.
    • Mix all the spice ingredients in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Marinate the Meat
    • Slice the meat thinly (for quick cooking).
    • In a bowl, mix the sliced meat with vegetable oil and sprinkle the suya spice generously.
    • Let the meat marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for a deeper flavor).
  3. Step 3: Skewer the Meat
    • Thread the marinated meat onto the skewers.
  4. Step 4: Grill the Suya
    • Oven Method:
    • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
    • Place the skewers on a wire rack and bake for 15-20 minutes, turning halfway.
    • For a smoky flavor, broil for an additional 5 minutes.
    • Charcoal Grill Method:
    • Place the skewers over hot charcoal and grill for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally until cooked and slightly charred.
  5. Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!
    • Serve the suya hot with sliced onions, tomatoes, cucumbers, and extra suya spice.
    • Enjoy with cold drinks, bread, or as a side dish.
  • Tips for the Best Suya:
  • ✔ Thin slices cook faster and absorb more flavor.
  • ✔ Marinate overnight for deeper taste.
  • ✔ Charcoal grilling gives the best authentic taste.
  • ✔ Serve fresh for the best experience!
