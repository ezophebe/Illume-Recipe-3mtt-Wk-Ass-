Efo-Riro Recipe:

Efo Riro is a delicious, rich spinach stew from Nigeria, particularly popular among the Yoruba people. It is made with leafy greens, assorted meats, and a flavorful pepper sauce.

Ingredients

  • The proteins:
    • 500g assorted meats (beef, tripe, cow skin, or chicken)
    • 2 smoked fish (e.g., mackerel or catfish), deboned
    • 1 cup dried prawns or shrimp (optional)
  • The Pepper sauce and Stew base:
    • 3 large red bell peppers (tatashe)
    • 2 medium tomatoes
    • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo) (adjust to taste)
    • 1 medium onion (for blending)
    • ½ cup palm oil
    • 1 medium onion (chopped)
    • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish (optional, but adds great flavor)
    • 2 tablespoons locust beans (iru)
    • 2 cups meat stock (from boiled beef, chicken, or assorted meat)
  • For the Greens:
    • 500g fresh spinach (or substitute with ugu or kale)
    • 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
    • 1 seasoning cube (Maggi or Knorr)
    • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional)

The coking steps:

  1. Blend the bell peppers, tomatoes, scotch bonnets, and one onion into a smooth paste.
  2. Wash and chop the spinach, then set aside.
  3. Boil the assorted meats with salt, seasoning cube, and onion until tender. Reserve the stock.
  4. Rinse the smoked fish and set aside.
  5. Heat the palm oil in a pot over medium heat
  6. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  7. Add the locust beans (iru) and stir for a minute.
  8. Pour in the blended pepper mix and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil begins to float on top.
  9. Step 3: Add Meat and Seasonings
  10. Add the cooked meats, smoked fish, and dried prawns.
  11. Pour in the reserved meat stock, add crayfish, and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  12. Season with salt, seasoning cube, and extra ground pepper if needed.
  13. Stir in the chopped spinach and allow it to cook for 3-5 minutes. The greens should be soft but still vibrant.
  14. Remove from heat and serve hot with rice, pounded yam, eba, fufu, or amala.
